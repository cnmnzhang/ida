IDA Burden in the
United States

Anemia-related DALYs in the US increased 26% from 332,449 in 1990 to 418,855 in 2019 — a troubling rise in a wealthy nation with food fortification programs. Female individuals account for nearly three-quarters of all US anemia DALYs, with dietary iron deficiency contributing more than half of total burden.

📊 What is a DALY?

A Disability-Adjusted Life Year (DALY) = 1 year of healthy life lost, either to premature death or to living with illness/disability. For IDA in the US, DALYs are almost entirely YLDs (years lived with disability) since anemia rarely causes direct death here. 418,855 DALYs in 2019 means roughly 419,000 full years of healthy life were erased that year — lost to fatigue, cognitive impairment, and reduced work capacity. Predominantly in women.

Overall US anemia prevalence (2021–23)

US anemia prevalence is 13.0% in females versus 5.5% in males overall (NHANES 2021–2023). Female adolescents ages 12–19 have the highest prevalence at 17.4% — reflecting the iron drain of menstruation onset with diets that rarely compensate. Black non-Hispanic women face an anemia prevalence of 31.4% — more than double the rate of non-Hispanic White women — the highest burden of any demographic group in the US.

US Anemia Prevalence by Sex — NHANES (1999–2023)
% of US population with anemia · sex-stratified estimates
Female rates have hovered at 12–13% with no meaningful decline since the early 2000s. IDA prevalence in pregnant US women trended from 6.63% (2015–16) to 9.9% (2021–23), with COVID-19 likely intensifying vulnerabilities around healthcare access and income stability.
US Anemia DALYs by Sex (1990–2019)
Thousands of healthy life years lost annually
In 2019, female individuals accounted for 166,741 DALYs versus 61,499 in males — nearly 3× more. Among ages 10–54, women had a severe anemia DALY rate approximately 10× higher than men for dietary iron deficiency specifically.
US Anemia DALYs by Cause (2019)
Proportional breakdown — what's driving health loss
Gynecological and obstetric causes are exclusively female contributors. Kidney disease and other chronic conditions grow with the aging population.

Anemia Prevalence by Race/Ethnicity & Sex
% with anemia · NHANES 2021–2023 · stark racial disparities persist
At the state level, Mississippi, the District of Columbia, and Alabama have the highest anemia-related DALY burden in the US — reflecting compounding geographic, racial, and socioeconomic inequities. Black non-Hispanic females face 31.4% prevalence versus 10.8% in Black non-Hispanic males, with disparate rates of hemoglobinopathies (sickle cell), dietary access, and screening gaps all contributing.
